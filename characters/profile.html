A formidable Rhyll warrior skilled in all forms of combat, Ghost is one of the Generals of the Rhyll Dominion. His citadel lies upon a snowy mountain to the North-east of the realm. In addition to his impressive combat skills, Ghost also has the ability to heal rapidly, to the extent that he can regenerate lost limbs, making him an even more daunting opponent. While fearsome in battle and merciless to his enemies, he is far more kind and sympathetic towards those under his rule. However, he despises traitors and will not hesitate to strike down those who have wronged him. He has no patience for incompetence and is disgusted by weak, cowardly warriors.

story

Youth

Ailithir lived for the most of his youth within the Illuminated City, the capital of the Lunaris realm. By his birthright, he was chosen to become the next Silver Sage (which is basically the cat pope/high priest of his race). As the first son of the prestigious and powerful Silver family, he led a happy and uneventful life, at least for the first ten years of his existence, studying and training under the former Silver Sage. However, when he was ten years old, his mother died giving birth to his youngest brother, Sakar. To make things worse, Sakar was born with pure black fur, which basically made him the Anti-Christ of Lunarian society. His father became paranoid that Sakar's existence would be discovered, which would not only bring disgrace to their proud family name, but also put Sakar at risk of being murdered by crazy superstitious Lunarians. He locked Sakar up in a small room within their mansion, charged Ailithir with the role of taking care of him, and made him promise to never let Sakar leave the house. Ailithir grew very close to Sakar, as he was the only person other than their father that Sakar had any interaction with. Ailithir often told Sakar stories of the beautiful outside world, which ignited a deep curiosity within the younger cat. When Sakar asked Ailithir to bring him out, Ailithir could not refuse. They did not wander far, and were soon found by their father. Their father, upon discovering that Ailithir had went against his wishes and put his brother's life at risk, flew into a rage and struck Ailithir. Ailithir, who previously had never been hit or punished, was deeply traumatized by this incident.

Main Story

Ailithir started spending more time away from home and in the company of his mentor, the former Silver Sage, who was a close family friend as well. Rumours of unrest within the neighbouring Rhyll Dominion began to spread, which culminated in a visit from the leaders of the Rhyll. The Rhyll asked for the Lunaris to spare them some land, for their territories were slowly being encroached on by a mysterious plague that left their earth barren and their water poisoned. However, the Lunaris declined their request, for their realm was already too small and would surely be overpopulated if they allowed the Rhyll to move in. Out of desperation, the Rhyll attacked the Lunaris realm, determined to seize the territory and save their people. As the Captain of the Lunaris Guard, Ailithir's father went away to join the war. However, the Lunaris forces were easily defeated by the far more combat-ready Rhyll. Eventually, the Rhyll army arrived at the Illuminated City, with Ailithir's father in tow as a prisoner. At the gates of the city, before an audience of horrified Lunaris (with Ailithir among them), they killed Ailithir's father and threatened the Lunaris to surrender, or they will do the same to the rest of them. The former Silver Sage ordered what was left of the Lunaris forces to defend the city, but it was a lost cause and the city was seized overnight. The Rhyll killed and imprisoned many members of the Lunaris noble families, but Ailithir was able to escape. Terrified, lost and completely helpless, Ailithir stumbled through the wilderness for weeks, trying to survive and evade capture. Eventually, Ailithir encountered a small group of deserters from the Rhyll Dominion, or rebels as they called themselves. They saved Ailithir from a Rhyll patrol and unaware of Ailithir's identity, they took him in, even though their leader, Caden, had his reservations. They traveled together for a few months, and Ailithir almost believed that he can start a new life with them, completely abandoning his devastated realm. However, mistakes were made and a majority of the group was captured by the Rhyll, leaving only Ailithir and Caden behind. Ailithir offered to help Caden to save his friends, and the two of them ventured to the nearest Rhyll outpost where they were sure the prisoners would be taken. Ailithir managed to help Caden's friends escape, but he gets captured himself. The Rhyll tortured him and demanded for him to tell them the location of a rumored secret Lunaris base, where a rebellion had begun. Ailithir, however, refused to tell them anything, so he was thrown back into a cell and left to await his execution.

The Future

After the war, Ailithir placed all his efforts into helping to rebuild his realm. Eventually, the realm started to prosper once more, entering a new Golden Age. Ailithir, finally able to return to his more passive role of being a priest, rebuilt his mansion into an orphanage for children orphaned during the war, and adopted a young orphaned girl by the name of Hilda. He settled down in a small cottage by the sea with his new family and all was well.

He wished.

Ailithir, on a diplomatic trip to the Hanwi Islands, encountered a Hanwi priestess. Both were fascinated by the others' powers, and after spending some time together, the two became very close. The priestess revealed herself to be Ieya, the Hanwi goddess of harvest. Ieya offered to create a child for him, a devastatingly powerful being who would be the culmination of both of their powers. Without understanding her real motives, Ailithir refused, but Ieya was adamant. One day, she simply vanished, leaving behind a demigod child on Ailithir's doorstep that Ailithir had no choice but to care for. As the Silver Sage, Ailithir was forbidden from marrying or having children, or else he was required to pass up his duties. Hence, when Ailithir returned with the child, Solius, the Lunarian council was outraged. However, since Ailithir had no apprentice, and there was no one suitable to replace him, he was pardoned. Solius, on the other hand, had to suffer through a childhood of prejudice and insults.

Ailithir loved Solius and raised him to the best he could. He secretly hoped that Solius would be the one to replace him as the next Silver Sage, but Solius adamantly refused to study magic, even though he demonstrated incredibly powerful magical abilities at a young age.

When Solius turned 14, a mysterious visitor arrived at the Illuminated City. The visitor, a self-proclaimed powerful sorcercer from the North started to teach Ailithir about dark magic and asked Ailithir to "join him in building a new world together". Slightly frightened, Ailithir rejected his offer. Things escalated and the sorcerer revealed himself to be Sakar, who had really been kidnapped and brought to the North, where he made a deal with a god to grant him powers beyond imagination should he do the god's bidding. Angry at Ailithir's rejection and believing that Ailithir had betrayed him once more, the sorcerer attacked Ailithir. At this point, Solius ran in to help his father, but the sorcerer only turned his attack on Solius. In trying to protect Solius, Ailithir is fatally wounded. With his dying breath, he gave Solius a locket containing a picture of Solius' mother, imploring Solius to seek her out.
